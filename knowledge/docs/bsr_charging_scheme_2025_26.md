# Building Safety Regulator Charging Scheme 2025 to 2026

**Source:** HSE / Building Safety Regulator
**Effective Date:** 1 April 2025
**Geographic Scope:** England only
**Document Type:** Charging Scheme

---

## Overview

The Building Safety Regulator (BSR) Charging Scheme 2025-2026 sets out the fees and charges for regulatory services provided under the Building Safety Act 2022 and Building Act 1984. The scheme operates on a cost recovery principle, meaning those who benefit from regulatory activities bear the associated costs.

This scheme supersedes the 2024-25 charging scheme and applies from 1 April 2025.

## Scope of Charges

The scheme contains 28 schedules covering different chargeable activities:

### Gateway Process (Higher-Risk Buildings)

- **Gateway 2 (Building Control Approval):** Charged at GBP 151/hour for assessment time
- **Gateway 3 (Completion Certificate):** GBP 227 fixed fee plus hourly assessment charges
- **Major revisions:** Hourly rate applies
- **Minor revisions:** Hourly rate applies

### HRB Registration and Occupation

- **HRB registration:** GBP 251 per building
- **Building assessment certificates:** GBP 302 application fee plus hourly assessment
- **Key building information updates:** Included in ongoing obligations

### Building Control Registration

- **Building inspector registration:** GBP 336 for 4-year registration period
- **Building control approver registration:** GBP 4,494 for 5-year registration period
- **Oversight activities:** GBP 130/hour

### Enforcement and Compliance

- Compliance notices
- Stop notices
- Enforcement investigations
- Appeals (separate schedule)

## Higher-Risk Buildings Definition

Buildings that qualify as higher-risk and are subject to BSR building control:

- **Height threshold:** 18 metres or more, OR 7+ storeys
- **AND** contains at least 2 residential units

**Also includes regardless of height:**
- Care homes
- Hospitals

---

## Payment Terms

- **Due date:** Within 30 days of invoice
- **Non-payment:** Recoverable as civil debt
- **Query process:** Three levels available:
  1. Initial query with BSR team
  2. Escalation to finance team
  3. Senior review

---

## Exceptions and Exemptions

### Disabled Persons Exemption

Building work carried out **solely** to facilitate the needs of a disabled person may be exempt from standard charges. The work must:
- Be solely for the benefit of a disabled person
- Relate directly to their disability

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Higher-Risk Building (HRB)** | Building 18m+ or 7+ storeys with 2+ residential units; or care home/hospital |
| **Principal Accountable Person (PAP)** | Person with overall responsibility for occupied HRB safety |
| **Client** | Person for whom building work is carried out |
| **Gateway 2** | Building control approval before construction starts |
| **Gateway 3** | Completion certificate before occupation |
| **Building Assessment Certificate** | Certificate confirming occupied HRB meets safety standards |

---

## Decision Flowchart

When using this charging scheme, consider:

1. **Is the building higher-risk?** (18m+/7+ storeys with residential units)
2. **What stage is the project?** (Pre-construction, construction, occupation)
3. **Who is the dutyholder?** (Client, PAP, building control body)
4. **Does the disabled persons exemption apply?**
5. **Is BSR the building control authority or is an RBC being used?**

- BSR charges GBP 151/hour for most building control assessments
- Oversight of building control bodies charged at GBP 130/hour

- Payment due within 30 days; recoverable as civil debt
