When you notify BSR about an emergency repair you must:
● describe the repair work
● explain the urgency and why it was not practical to apply for building control approval
before carrying out the repair work
● describe any measures put in place to reduce the urgent risks until the repair work is
finished
As soon as the work starts you must send a copy of the emergency repair information to the
responsible person under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005. You must also keep a
copy of the emergency repair information as part of the information you must keep about a building.
BSR will assess the emergency repair information you provide. If BSR decides that the work:
● meets the criteria for an emergency repair, BSR will tell you what to do next
● does not meet the criteria for an emergency repair, then the work is unauthorised and
BSR will decide if they need to take any action
After emergency repair work is completed
After the emergency repair work is completed you must apply for a regularisation certificate as soon
as possible. You can do this by contacting BSR.
The charges for applying for a regularisation certificate are set out in the BSR charging scheme.
Additional reporting requirements for high-rise residential buildings
The principal accountable person (PAP) must update the building’s safety case report to include the
emergency repair work. If you are not the PAP, you must send a copy of the emergency repair notice
to the PAP.
The relevant accountable person must submit a mandatory occurrence notice and report to BSR if
both of the following apply:
● the repair work was carried out to manage risks relating to structural failure or the spread
of fire in the building
