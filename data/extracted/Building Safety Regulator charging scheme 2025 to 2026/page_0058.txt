4-step process
To set charges, HSE follows a 4-step activity-based costing process to cost the direct time
required to deliver the relevant direct recoverable activities.
1: Salary costs
Identify the total forecast volume of effort and total payroll cost (salary, employers’ national
insurance and employers’ superannuation) of the individuals involved in the direct delivery of the
activity. This is calculated as an average hourly rate per grade and assumes a total of 220
available days in a working year allowing for weekends, leave and other absences.
2: Attributable costs
This is costed staff effort that is directly linked to the chargeable activity and is material enough
to justify direct attribution of the costs (for example, training and development effort linked to a
specific capability, management oversight, guidance, cost of travel time, and direct
administrative and management support).
3: Indirect costs
