The oversight of building control as a regulated profession will monitor the performance of all
building control bodies, and the competence of registered building inspectors and registered
building control approvers, to ensure consistency and drive-up standards. The regime also
provides assurance and supports shared learning across the built environment.
Section 28 of the act and section 105B of the Building Act 1984 provide the powers for BSR to
make and recover charges in connection with the performance of a relevant function. These
chargeable functions are defined in the Building Safety (Regulator’s Charges) Regulations 2023
(the regulations). Under the powers outlined, the charges recoverable by BSR for performing
the chargeable functions may be set out in a charging scheme. This document fulfils that
requirement.
In line with HSE strategy, the costs of these services are recovered through charges to make
sure those benefiting from the service bear the cost. This ensures a proportionate approach
where those whose activities need the highest level of intervention and oversight will pay the
highest cost.
BSR charges and how they are to be calculated are as set out in this document unless it is
replaced by a later published version of the charging scheme.
